Education and Training

Gerry is a lifelong learner. After surviving Kamloops Residential School, he went on to gain training in traditional ways of knowing, being, and doing as well as in counselling and human services.

Traditional Education

  • Personal Vision Quests: 2002 (Coast Salish), 1995 (St'at'imc), 1975 (St'at'imc)
  • Vision Quest Assistant, 1980 and 2003-present (Coast Salish)
  • Six month residential program with Elders, Fountain, British Columbia 1979 (St'at'imc)
  • Ecumenical Conference for First Nations Healers, a week long residential program in ceremonies and teachings, Morley, Alberta, 1978
  • Sweat Ceremony Training, 1976 (St'at'imc)
  • First Nations Death and Dying Rituals and Ceremonies with Elders, 1976 (St'at'imc)

Traditional Skills

  • Ceremonies (sweat lodge, funerals, weddings)
  • Traditional Blessings (objects such as drums, ceremonial locations, structures)
  • Traditional Tools and Cultural Objects (dip nets, fishing spear, pipe stems, rattles)
  • Traditional Resource Use (fish and game harvesting and management, plant use, resource management including watershed systems, controlled burn areas, medicines, traditional preparation and storage of food)
  • Traditional Child Rearing Practices (parenting, name giving, baptisms, rites of passage)
  • Traditional Oration (traditional leadership skills, public speaking, decision-making processes, and protocols)
  • Traditional Grieving Practices
  • North American First Nations Legends and Mythology Recounting
  • Traditional Hand Drum Music (social and ceremonial)
